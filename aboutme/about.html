Born and raised in Miami, Amanda Alvarez is a recent graduate from the University of Miami.

She has always been interested in television; it all began at Ethel Koger Beckham Elementary School where she started working on the television program called the "Best News." Every morning she reported on the lunch menu and afterschool activities that were scheduled to take place.

Her passion for journalism expanded when she started middle school where she frequently wrote for the school's newspaper and worked on the yearbook during her time there.

Meanwhile, Amanda has been involved in cheerleading and dancing since the age of five. She has competed at many competitions locally and nationally, receiving accolades at almost all of them. Her talent extends beyond the dance floor. She also cheered competitively through her elementary and middle school career, as well as cheered for her high school's football and basketball teams. She was a a cheerleader at the University of Miami and a member of the KAOS hip-hop team.

All while juggling extra curriculars and a full course load at Miami, she interned with the UM Athletic Department. She has gained expereince working different roles within the communication field. From beat writing, to feature stories, to sideline reporting, she thrive in an environment where no two work days are exactly the same. She has also worked in mock newsroom situations to help produce Sports Desk and News Vision, two award winning University of Miami Television shows.
